## Codebase Patterns
- Team Analysis Summary UI lives primarily in `components/organisms/AnalysisPanel/AnalysisPanel.tsx`; keep client components display-only and do not recompute optimizer scoring in the UI.
- Threat Score diagnostics are exposed through `OptimizerScoreBreakdown.threatScore`; keep backend fields such as rank, team answers, and threat value available for sorting and tests, but do not show those raw details in the user-facing Threat Score list for this PRD.
- Threat Score UI should show readable team weaknesses and one team-level threat profile pill; raw Overall, Top Meta, Full Meta, Risk, Rank, and Answers numbers should not be user-facing in the Threat Score card.
- Recommended Lineups belongs in the `AnalysisPanel` accordion immediately after Summary Statistics; keep Lead, Switch, Closer, and semantic weakness lists, but do not render weak/neutral/strong/elite quality pills for this PRD.
- Summary Statistics optimizer score cards should render only simple `A`, `B`, `C`, `D`, or `F` letter grades for score components; do not render numeric optimizer component scores, plus/minus modifiers, or quality pills in the component cards.
- Optimizer generation changes must use format-specific ranking data from `lib/data/rankings.ts`; do not hardcode open Great League score thresholds as global viability bands.
- Dynamic candidate bands should be derived per format from score distribution, rank percentile, score density, score dropoffs, and bounded candidate counts.
- Use `deriveCandidateRankingBands(...)` in `lib/data/candidateRankingBands.ts` for pure ranking-band logic and `getCandidateRankingBands(...)` in `lib/data/rankings.ts` for format-scoped overall ranking data; do not hardcode PvPoke scores like 92, 90, 88, or 85 as global viability bands.
- Use `buildCandidateProfiles(...)` in `lib/data/candidateProfiles.ts` to combine dynamic band assignments with injected species, role, consistency, move typing, and simulation coverage inputs; keep candidate profiles pure and avoid file reads in generation or scoring code.
- Use `rankAnchorCompanionPairs(...)` in `lib/data/companionPairRanking.ts` to evaluate pure anchor plus companion candidate-profile pairs; keep rank and PvPoke score as priors while simulation coverage, unique anchor-loss coverage, safety, consistency, bulk, typing, and shared weakness penalties drive the pair score.
- Anchor-first generation should use `initializeAnchorFirstPopulation(...)` in `lib/genetic/chromosome.ts` to seed elite or preferred anchors, rank anchor plus companion pairs, expand teams by remaining weaknesses and lineup quality, preserve a bounded random diversity slice, then rely on canonical lineup or roster scoring for final fitness.
- Anchor-first profile construction should use the automatic non-specialist candidate pool plus explicit `anchorPokemon` ranking names; keep random initialization and mutation pools restricted to `getAutomaticCandidatePokemonNames(...)` output so pinned specialists do not leak into automatic generation.
- Anchor-first candidate profile coverage should evaluate wins/losses against a format-scoped meta threat pool such as `getRoleBasedThreatSpeciesIds(...)`, not only against the automatic generation pool.
- Anchor-first seeding should preserve at least one ranked seed when `populationSize > 0` and candidate profiles are available; the random diversity reserve must not consume the whole population.
- Anchor-first expansion should consider remaining weaknesses first, then profile-backed lineup quality and broad meta wins/checks as tie-break signals; reject non-explicit specialist companions even if a custom pair ranker returns them.
- Low-ranked specialists should be admitted only with unique measurable top-meta or core-weakness coverage and should not crowd out safer high-ranked generalists without matchup evidence.
- Use `evaluateSpecialistAdmission(...)` in `lib/data/specialistCandidateGate.ts` for dynamic-band `specialists`; require unique simulation-backed unresolved threat or core-weakness coverage, reject isolated or duplicated coverage, and keep specialists out of automatic anchors.
- Deduplicate unresolved threat context by Pokemon identity before weighting specialist admission so duplicate matchup rows cannot make isolated specialist coverage look unique or high-priority.
- Use `getAutomaticCandidatePokemonNames(...)` in `lib/data/rankings.ts` for automatic generation pools so dynamic-band specialists are excluded before random team initialization, while explicit user-provided anchors remain allowed through `anchorPokemon`.
- Runtime app, component, and optimizer code must not import, require, bundle, or load PvPoke vendor JavaScript; keep local PvPoke engine execution isolated to sync tooling.
- For optimizer-adjacent changes, invoke/read the `gbl-optimizer` skill and record optimizer docs read in this file before marking the story complete.
